About NMG-VRG

A collaborative platform for genomics-driven discovery.

NMG-VRG is an international, virtual research network bringing together emerging scientists with shared interests in microbial genomics and computational biology. The group provides a collaborative environment for applying genome sequencing, bioinformatics, and data-driven approaches to microbial pathogens.

Our work emphasizes infectious diseases, antimicrobial resistance, and global health, with particular relevance to resource-limited settings where genomic evidence can directly strengthen surveillance, preparedness, and policy.

How We Work

Fully virtual, globally distributed, project-based, inclusive, and merit-driven. Genomic and artificial intelligence projects rely on sequence datasets and metadata retrieved from publicly available repositories and databases.

Code of Conduct

Professionalism, respect, ethical research, responsible data use, and scientific integrity.

Mission

Amplifying the ideas and skills of young scientists.

To advance microbial genomics and computational biology through collaborative, rigorous research addressing infectious diseases, antimicrobial resistance, and global health.

Vision

Building a globally connected research community.

To create a virtual ecosystem where early-career researchers lead impactful genomic studies that inform public health action and policy, especially in resource-limited settings.

Research Focus Areas

Core thematic domains.

Antimicrobial Resistance Genomics

Tracking resistance determinants, evolution, and genomic epidemiology of priority pathogens.

Pathogen Surveillance

Outbreak analysis, transmission mapping, and surveillance systems strengthened by genomics.

Comparative Genomics

Population structure, lineage diversity, adaptation, and evolutionary inference.

Metagenomics & Microbiome

Community-level analysis of microbes across clinical, environmental, and One Health settings.

Artificial Intelligence Applications

Predictive and data-driven methods for genomic interpretation and biological discovery.

One Health

Integrated studies spanning human, animal, and environmental interfaces.
