Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about NMG-VRG

1. What is NMG-VRG?

The NextGen Microbial Genomics Virtual Research Group (NMG-VRG) is an international research community that connects students, early-career scientists, and young professionals working in microbial genomics, bioinformatics, and infectious disease research.

2. Who can join the community?

Membership is open to undergraduate students, graduate students, early-career researchers, and professionals with an interest in microbial genomics, computational biology, antimicrobial resistance, and global health research.

3. Do members need prior bioinformatics experience?

No. While most projects require bioinformatics skills, the community provides training sessions, workshops, and mentorship opportunities to help members develop these skills.

4. How are research projects organized?

Projects are organized into project-based working groups. Each group focuses on a specific research question and operates under the coordination of project leaders and scientific advisors. The projects are initially designed by the core team and published on the website to attract individuals who would like to contribute.

5. Is NMG-VRG a physical institution?

No. NMG-VRG operates entirely as a virtual research community, enabling collaboration across countries and institutions through online platforms.

6. Are members able to contribute to publications?

Yes. All members must contribute to research manuscripts, conference abstracts, and scientific reports within the assigned project.

7. Are there training opportunities?

Yes. The community organizes workshops, journal clubs, and bioinformatics training sessions designed to build research capacity among members.

8. How can someone apply for membership?

Applicants can apply through the membership form available on the website. Applications are reviewed when positions are available within active research projects. So applicants are encouraged to check the website regularly for updates on project openings and application deadlines.

9. Does membership require a fee?

Currently, membership is free. However, participation requires commitment to collaborative research activities and adherence to the community's code of conduct.

10. How can organizations collaborate with NMG-VRG?

Institutions and researchers interested in collaboration may contact the NMG-VRG team through the official email listed on the website.
